ANSYS for Mechanical Engineers

Simulation training for mechanical engineers working on power electronics products. SpaceClaim, Workbench, Mechanical, Icepak, and Maxwell — from geometry preparation through coupled multi-physics analysis.

Sections

From geometry to multi-physics

Each section builds on the last. Geometry preparation is the foundation — every analysis downstream depends on getting it right.

  1. Section 01

    Foundations

    The ANSYS product landscape, how to use the customer training portal without getting misled by older-version content, importing geometry from CAD into SpaceClaim, and what goes wrong when models are too complex.

    • ANSYS Workbench
    • SpaceClaim import
    • CAD workflow
    • Model complexity
  2. Section 02

    SpaceClaim Model Preparation

    Geometry simplification for structural, thermal, and electromagnetic analyses. Remove mesh-hostile features, create fluid volumes, and size air regions — working from a real STEP file throughout.

    • SpaceClaim
    • Defeaturing
    • Fluid volumes
    • Air region sizing
  3. Section 03

    Workbench and Meshing

    The Workbench project workflow, mesh quality metrics, sizing strategies and rules of thumb, and the iterative cycle between mesh failure and geometry repair.

    • ANSYS Workbench
    • Mesh quality
    • Sizing strategy
    • Failure recovery
  4. Section 04

    Structural and Thermal Analyses

    Static structural, modal analysis and random vibration, steady-state thermal with CTE-driven stress, and plastic deformation with nonlinear material models.

    • Static structural
    • Modal & RSD
    • Thermal-structural
    • Plastic deformation
  5. Section 05

    Icepak, Maxwell, and Multi-Physics

    Thermal CFD with ANSYS Icepak — airflow, power sources, compact models, and parametric heatsink studies. Electromagnetic analysis with ANSYS Maxwell — DC conduction, eddy current losses, and transformer modeling. Coupled EM-to-thermal workflows.

    • ANSYS Icepak
    • ANSYS Maxwell
    • CFD
    • EM losses
    • Multi-physics
