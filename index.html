Internal Training Program

ANSYS for Mechanical Engineers

Hands-on simulation training for mechanical engineers working on power electronics products. Every section follows the same structure: introduce the problem, examine a real example, build the model, review what the results mean.

2.5 days 5 sections SpaceClaim · Workbench · Icepak · Maxwell Under development

Curriculum

Five sections, built to build capability

Each section starts with geometry — the one thing everything else depends on — and builds toward multi-physics workflows. Sections are designed to stand alone; the instructor selects depth based on what the team needs.

  1. 1

    Foundations

    The ANSYS product landscape, how to use the customer training portal without getting misled by older-version content, importing geometry from CAD into SpaceClaim, and what goes wrong when models are too complex.

    • ANSYS Workbench
    • SpaceClaim import
    • CAD workflow
    • Model complexity
  2. 2

    SpaceClaim Model Preparation

    Geometry simplification for three analysis types. Practice on a real STEP file: remove mesh-hostile features for structural, create fluid volumes for thermal, and size air regions for electromagnetics.

    • SpaceClaim
    • Defeaturing
    • Fluid volumes
    • Air region sizing
  3. 3

    Workbench and Meshing

    The Workbench project workflow, mesh quality metrics, sizing strategies and rules of thumb, and the iterative cycle between mesh failure and geometry repair.

    • ANSYS Workbench
    • Mesh quality
    • Sizing strategy
    • Failure recovery
  4. 4

    Structural and Thermal Analyses

    Static structural, modal analysis and random vibration, steady-state thermal with CTE-driven stress, and plastic deformation with nonlinear material models.

    • Static structural
    • Modal & RSD
    • Thermal-structural
    • Plastic deformation
  5. 5

    Icepak, Maxwell, and Multi-Physics

    Thermal CFD with ANSYS Icepak — airflow, power sources, compact models, and parametric heatsink studies. Electromagnetic analysis with ANSYS Maxwell — DC conduction, eddy current losses, and transformer modeling. Coupled EM-to-thermal workflows.

    • ANSYS Icepak
    • ANSYS Maxwell
    • CFD
    • EM losses
    • Multi-physics

How every section is structured

Introduction, example, hands-on, results

01

Introduce the problem

What engineering situation does this analysis address, and when should you use it?

02

Real example

The instructor walks through a real project — what worked, what failed, and why.

03

Hands-on build

Attendees build or modify the model themselves. Watching is not the same as doing.

04

Review results

What do the numbers mean for the design? What would change your conclusion?
